What is Blood Donation?

The Gift of Life in 450 mL

Blood donation is a voluntary process where a healthy individual gives blood that is later used for blood transfusions or made into medications. India requires approximately 14.6 million units of blood every year, but less than half is collected.


Blood cannot be manufactured — it can only come from generous human donors. Whether for surgeries, cancer treatment, childbirth complications, or accidents, donated blood is irreplaceable.

Types of Blood Donation
💉

Whole Blood

The most common type. One unit can be separated into red cells, plasma, and platelets — helping up to 3 patients.

🔬

Platelets (Apheresis)

Critical for cancer & chemotherapy patients. Platelets only last 5 days, making regular donors vital.

🧪

Plasma Donation

Plasma carries clotting factors and is used for burn victims, trauma patients, and immune disorders.

Eligibility Criteria

Are You Eligible to Donate?

✅

Age: 18–65 years

Must be between 18 and 65 years of age. First-time donors above 60 need physician approval.

✅

Weight: ≥ 45 kg

Minimum weight of 45 kg is required to ensure donor safety during the donation process.

✅

Haemoglobin ≥ 12.5 g/dL

Minimum hemoglobin level required to prevent anaemia after donation. Tested before each donation.

✅

Blood Pressure Normal

Systolic 100–180 mmHg and Diastolic 60–100 mmHg with no active hypertension treatment.

✅

Pulse: 60–100 bpm

Regular pulse rate and no history of fainting or dizziness during past donations.

✅

Good Health

Free from cold, flu, or any infection on the day of donation. Well-rested with a good meal before donating.

Temporary & Permanent Deferrals

Who Should Not Donate?

⛔

Pregnant or nursing mothers — wait 6 months after delivery or 3 months after stopping breastfeeding.

⛔

Recent tattoo or piercing — defer for 6 months to 1 year depending on facility conditions.

⛔

HIV, Hepatitis B/C, or active STIs — permanent deferral for safety of recipient blood supply.

⛔

Recent surgery or dental work — minimum 6-month deferral post major surgery, 1 week for dental.

⛔

Malaria history — 3-month deferral after recovery from malaria infection.

⛔

Drug use (intravenous) — permanent deferral due to high risk of blood-borne disease transmission.

⛔

Active cancer treatment — individuals on chemotherapy or radiation therapy must defer.

⛔

Recent vaccination — some vaccines require 2–4 week deferral (e.g., MMR, typhoid, flu).

Health Benefits

What You Gain by Donating

❤️ Better Heart Health

Regular donation reduces the risk of heart attacks and strokes by lowering blood viscosity and iron levels that cause oxidative stress.

🩺 Free Health Checkup

Before each donation, you receive a mini health screening — pulse, blood pressure, haemoglobin, and blood-borne disease testing at no cost.

🔄 Cell Regeneration

Donating stimulates production of new blood cells. Your body completely regenerates the donated blood within 4–8 weeks.

🧠 Mental Wellbeing

The act of giving triggers release of endorphins. Donors consistently report higher happiness and a powerful sense of purpose.

⚖️ Weight Awareness

Donating 450 mL burns approximately 650 calories. It also promotes awareness of maintaining healthy body weight for eligibility.

🛡️ Reduced Cancer Risk

Studies suggest regular blood donation may reduce the risk of liver cancer and haemochromatosis by managing excess iron stores.

Before & After Donation

Tips for a Safe Donation

🥗

Eat Before Donating

Have a nutritious meal 2–3 hours before donation. Avoid fatty foods but eat iron-rich foods like spinach, lentils, and eggs.

💧

Stay Hydrated

Drink at least 500 mL of extra water before donating. Good hydration makes the process faster and reduces dizziness.

😴

Rest Well

Get at least 7–8 hours of sleep the night before. Fatigue can affect your eligibility and post-donation recovery.

🚫

Avoid Alcohol

Do not consume alcohol for at least 24 hours before donating. Alcohol dehydrates and affects blood quality.

🛋️

Rest After

Sit or lie down for 10–15 minutes after donation. Avoid strenuous activity or heavy lifting for a few hours.

🍊

Replenish Nutrients

After donating, eat iron-rich and vitamin C foods to help rebuild red blood cells. Your body fully recovers in 4–6 weeks.
